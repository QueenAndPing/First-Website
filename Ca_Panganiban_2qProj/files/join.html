A Call to Action

Why BMJ?

The BMJ can be many things to its diverse audiences: an international research journal, a member magazine, a think tank, an investigative campaigner, an educational resource, even a trusted and inspiring friend. Whatever The BMJ means to you, we invite you to share our mission and join us in working towards a healthier world for all.

Why I chose BMJ...

My goals meet end to end with BMJ's. Their goal is to be a source of research and medical information to all, while my goal is to promote research on the many more possibilities to save lives. While it may seem a bit different, what matters is that our immediate goal is to help rescue people the best way we can. That is why I chose BMJ.

What is our advocacy?

Our advocacy is to promote and increase allocation for medical research to develop better remedies and cures.

Why our advocacy?

Our advocacy for medical research is beneficial because it can lead to the development of new treatments and cures for diseases.
